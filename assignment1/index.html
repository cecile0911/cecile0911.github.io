Interactive Experience

Research Project

Frank Gehry and the Walt Disney Concert Hall

By Cecile Yang

s4121253

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I paid attention to was the text description on the website.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

After reading the website text, I moved my mouse and noticed that the large letters “Sculpture Harmony” distorted as I moved the cursor. I then right-clicked and tried dragging it, which caused the webpage to change colors and display text following the mouse’s movement.

After that, I found the Menu button and browsed through the chapters in order, which included videos. Before the first chapter finished playing, I used the menu to return to the main interface, then scrolled according to the text prompts, discovering that scrolling also allowed me to switch between chapters one to three.

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

While scrolling to switch pages, 3D images of the Disney Concert Hall appeared from different angles. This experience was very interesting, and I watched it several times.

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

Using the navigation menu.

5.What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

I think the primary goal of the website is to allow users to immerse themselves in the story of Frank Gehry and the Disney Concert Hall.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

Through harmonious use of colors and the selection of symphonic music as background audio.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

The website gives me a linear impression. Since the information provided is not extremely varied, the presentation feels like an immersive reading experience with music and videos. It is suitable for slow exploration, possibly in sessions of ten minutes or more.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The website has a clear chapter structure, with each chapter containing a video that can be divided into segments which can be fast-forwarded by clicking. The videos themselves are not very short, which encourages slow and thoughtful exploration.

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The design of the website evokes the layout and visual style of a magazine, with carefully arranged sections and imagery that resemble editorial spreads. Within the site, there are embedded videos, interactive elements that function like dynamic photos which can be zoomed in and out using the mouse scroll wheel, and background music that enhances the atmosphere. These references combine both digital and traditional media forms to create a rich, immersive experience.

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

The references to magazine-style layouts, embedded videos, and interactive dynamic photos suggest that I should actively explore the content at my own pace. I am encouraged to scroll, click, and zoom to examine details, watch the videos, and navigate different sections thoughtfully rather than passively scrolling through the site.

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

These media references create a sense of immersion and engagement, inviting curiosity and attentiveness. The magazine-like presentation and background music set a reflective, contemplative mood, encouraging me to feel interested, inspired, and connected to Gehry’s work while exploring the experience.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

Although the dynamic photo design is impressive, the zoom speed of the photos is very fast regardless of how slowly I scroll the mouse wheel, which gives me a strong sense of dizziness. This part of the interaction could benefit from further refinement.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

I really enjoy the website’s design elements, especially the homepage’s use of monochrome and clean sketch lines to depict shapes, which gives a unique and sophisticated impression. The text that follows the mouse cursor is also very creative, and the use of sans-serif fonts is perfectly balanced and avant-garde.
